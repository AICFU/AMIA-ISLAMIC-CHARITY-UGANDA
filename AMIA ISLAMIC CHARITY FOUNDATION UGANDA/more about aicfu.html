Our Story

History: Humble Beginnings (2019)

Amia Islamic Charity Foundation was born out of compassion and conviction in Bulambuli, Eastern Uganda. A group of visionary elders from Siipi Main Mosque, led by Sheikh Adam, recognized the dire needs of their community. Orphans, widows, and vulnerable families struggled to access basic necessities.

The Spark

In 2019, the elders decided to take action. They pooled resources, expertise, and passion to establish Amia Islamic Charity Foundation. The name "Amia" reflects the Swahili word for "peace" and "security," embodying the foundation's mission.

Early Milestones

  • Initial Fundraising (2019)
  • First Orphanage Support (2020)
  • Widow Empowerment Program (2020)

Growth and Expansion (2020-Present)

  • Supported over 500 beneficiaries.
  • Partnered with local organizations and international donors.
  • Established a vocational training center.
  • Provided emergency aid during COVID-19 and natural disasters.

Legacy and Vision

Today, Amia Islamic Charity Foundation stands as a beacon of hope in Eastern Uganda. Their vision remains clear: a compassionate society where everyone thrives.
